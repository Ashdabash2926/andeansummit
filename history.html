Everything you need to know about Huaraz, the Cordillera Blanca, and travelling in Peru.

Altitude

Huaraz sits at 3 100 m in the Callejón de Huaylas, the valley between the Cordillera Blanca and Cordillera Negra. Most visitors need two to three days to acclimatise before heading higher.

Climate & season

The dry season runs June through August — the optimal window for trekking and climbing. May and September are also enjoyable but you should expect occasional rain showers.

Getting there

By road: 400 km from Lima on the main paved route (≈8 h by overnight bus). By air: a 30-minute flight from Lima's Jorge Chávez to Anta airport, then 20 minutes by road.

Flora & fauna

Glaciated peaks above 6 000 m, glacial lakes, hot springs, the giant Puya Raimondi (the world's largest bromeliad) and Andean condors.

Geography

Peru spans coast, Andes and Amazon. The Cordillera Blanca is the highest tropical mountain range on earth, with 33 peaks above 5 500 m within a 180 km stretch.

Currency

Peruvian Sol (PEN). ATMs are widely available in Huaraz; bring small notes for rural villages. USD is accepted by most agencies.

Language

Spanish is the national language; Quechua is widely spoken in the Andes. Our guides operate in English, Spanish and French.

Visa

Most European, North American, Australian and New Zealand citizens enter Peru visa-free for up to 183 days. Always confirm requirements with your embassy before travel.
